## What Exactly Is a Brazilian Wax?

A Brazilian wax removes hair from the front, sides, underneath, and between the cheeks. You can customize the coverage based on your preference — from a full removal to leaving a small strip or triangle in front. Unlike bikini waxes that only tidy the bikini line, a Brazilian gives you complete smoothness that lasts 3-4 weeks. The results are cleaner and longer-lasting than shaving, with less risk of razor burn or bumps.

## Why Choose Professional Brazilian Waxing?

Professional waxers use hard wax designed for sensitive areas, which grips only the hair — not the skin — making removal more comfortable. Licensed studios like Waxology Studio follow strict sanitation protocols, including fresh gloves and sanitized tools for every client.

## How to Prepare for Your First Brazilian Wax

Let hair grow to about 1/4 inch — the length of a grain of rice. This typically means avoiding shaving for 2-3 weeks. The day before your appointment, gently exfoliate the area and take a warm shower to soften hair follicles.

## What to Expect During Your Brazilian Wax

Your technician will discuss your preferences and explain the process. Hard wax is applied in small sections, allowed to harden, and then quickly removed against the direction of hair growth. First-time Brazilians typically take 30-45 minutes.

## Does a Brazilian Wax Hurt?

There is some discomfort, but it's often not as bad as first-timers expect. The pain is brief and sharp, and using hard wax significantly reduces the sting. Most clients find that subsequent visits are much easier as hair grows back finer.

## Aftercare for Your Brazilian Wax

For the first 24-48 hours, avoid hot baths, saunas, tanning, and intense workouts. Wear loose cotton underwear and skip scented products in the waxed area. Starting 2-3 days after your wax, begin gentle exfoliation to prevent ingrown hairs.
